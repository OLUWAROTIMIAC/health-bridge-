Infectious Diseases

Influenza (Flu)

A viral infection that attacks the respiratory system.

Symptoms

Fever Cough Sore throat Muscle aches

Causes

Influenza viruses types A, B, and C. Spread through respiratory droplets.

Treatments

Oseltamivir (Tamiflu) Zanamivir (Relenza) Rest & fluids

Annual vaccination is the best prevention.

Tuberculosis (TB)

A serious bacterial infection that mainly affects the lungs.

Symptoms

Persistent cough Chest pain Weight loss Night sweats

Causes

Mycobacterium tuberculosis bacteria. Spread through airborne particles.

Treatments

Isoniazid Rifampin Ethambutol Pyrazinamide

Treatment typically lasts 6-9 months. DOT (Directly Observed Therapy) is recommended.

Chronic Conditions

Type 2 Diabetes

A chronic condition that affects the way the body processes blood sugar (glucose).

Symptoms

Increased thirst Frequent urination Fatigue Blurred vision

Causes

Insulin resistance, often related to obesity, inactivity, and genetic factors.

Treatments

Metformin (Glucophage) Sulfonylureas Insulin therapy Lifestyle changes

Regular blood sugar monitoring is essential. Can lead to serious complications if unmanaged.

Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

A condition where the force of blood against artery walls is too high.

Symptoms

Often asymptomatic Headaches Shortness of breath Nosebleeds

Causes

Multiple factors including genetics, obesity, salt sensitivity, aging, and lifestyle.

Treatments

ACE inhibitors Beta-blockers Diuretics Calcium channel blockers

Lifestyle changes (DASH diet, exercise) can significantly improve condition.

Mental Health Conditions

Major Depressive Disorder

A mood disorder causing persistent feelings of sadness and loss of interest.

Symptoms

Persistent sadness Loss of interest Fatigue Sleep disturbances

Causes

Combination of genetic, biological, environmental, and psychological factors.

Treatments

SSRIs (e.g., Fluoxetine/Prozac) SNRIs Psychotherapy ECT (severe cases)

Treatment often combines medication and therapy. Suicide risk requires immediate attention.

Generalized Anxiety Disorder

Excessive, uncontrollable worry about everyday things.

Symptoms

Excessive worry Restlessness Fatigue Muscle tension

Causes

Genetic predisposition, brain chemistry, personality, and life events.

Treatments

SSRIs Benzodiazepines (short-term) CBT Mindfulness therapy

Benzodiazepines can be habit-forming. CBT is highly effective long-term.

Neurological Disorders

Alzheimer's Disease

A progressive neurodegenerative disease that destroys memory and cognitive function.

Symptoms

Memory loss Confusion Difficulty communicating Personality changes

Causes

Plaques and tangles in the brain, genetic factors, and environmental influences.

Treatments

Donepezil (Aricept) Memantine (Namenda) Cognitive therapy

Current treatments can't stop progression but may slow symptoms. Supportive care is crucial.

Parkinson's Disease

A progressive nervous system disorder that affects movement.

Symptoms

Tremors Stiffness Slow movement Balance problems

Causes

Loss of dopamine-producing neurons in the brain. Genetic and environmental factors play a role.

Treatments

Levodopa/Carbidopa Dopamine agonists Deep brain stimulation Physical therapy

Medications become less effective over time. Multidisciplinary approach is best.
